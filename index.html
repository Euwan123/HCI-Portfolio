SmartNutri

HCI Final Project

SmartNutri helps users log meals, scan food items, and get nutrition guidance from an AI assistant. I built the interface around usability testing and iterative prototyping so everyday tracking stays simple.

Objectives

  • Simplify meal logging with visual food scanning
  • Provide accessible nutrition insights through an AI assistant
  • Apply HCI principles to reduce friction in daily tracking
  • Validate flows through user testing and heuristic review

My Role

Lead designer and developer. I handled user research, wireframes, UI design, prototyping, and the project documentation.

CCIS InnoVision 2026

Best Software Architecture

SmartNutri received the Best Software Architecture award at the CCIS InnoVision showcase for its clear system flow, organized features, and practical use of HCI principles across the app.

My Reflection

I honestly did not expect to win Best Software Architecture. I kept redoing the food scanning flow because I was worried users would get confused. When the judges said the app felt structured and easy to follow, it made all the late nights worth it. This award reminded me that good software is not just about looking nice. It should make sense the moment someone opens it.
